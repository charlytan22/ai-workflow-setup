Collected Content and Summaries for AI SEO Experts

1. Ross Simmonds (Foundation / Distribution.ai)

• LinkedIn Post (Feb 14, 2026): "Marketers need AI-proof content."

• Summary: Discusses the shift from "more content" (2020) to "better content" (2022) to "AI-proof content" (2024-2026).

• Main Ideas: Content must have "Information Gain" and unique perspectives to survive AI scraping and commoditization.

• Tactics: Focus on proprietary data, case studies, and storytelling that AI cannot easily replicate.

• AI Workflow: Using AI to identify content gaps and distribution opportunities rather than just generation.



2. Kevin Indig (Growth Memo)

• Newsletter/Post (Jan 5, 2026): "State of AI Search Optimization 2026."

• Summary: Guide on how search is shifting from ranked lists to definitive answers.

• Main Ideas: Retrieval, citation, and trust are the new pillars. "Metrics are broken" (Dec 2025) - traditional CTR is less relevant than "Brand Mentions" in AI answers.

• Frameworks: The "AEO/GEO Optimization Loop" - Monitor, Optimize, Measure brand presence in LLMs.

• AI Workflow: Real-time optimization guidance for both SEO and AI search visibility.



3. Bernard Huang (Clearscope)

• Webinar/YouTube (2024-2025): "How to Rank SEO Content in the Era of Generative AI."

• Summary: Focuses on "Information Gain" as the primary ranking factor in the AI era.

• Main Ideas: If your content says the same thing as the top 10 results, it has zero information gain and won't be cited by AI.

• Tactics: Add unique data, contrarian views, or expert quotes to every piece of content.

• SEO Recommendations: Optimize for "Search Intent" but prioritize "User Experience" and "Trust Signals."



4. Chima Mmeje (Moz / Zenith Copy)

• Summary: Building story-led "how-to" content from events and webinars using AI.

• Tactics: Turning webinar transcripts into multiple content assets (LinkedIn posts, blogs, newsletters) using AI to maintain the "expert voice."

• AI Workflow: Human-in-the-loop (HITL) workflows where AI handles the heavy lifting of drafting while humans provide the unique narrative.



5. Mike King (iPullRank)

• Keynote/Post (2025-2026): "The End of SEO As We Know It / Relevance Engineering."

• Summary: Explains how AI Overviews source answers from LinkedIn, YouTube, and Quora.

• Main Ideas: "Relevance Engineering" - making your site technically accessible and highly relevant to LLM retrieval systems.

• Tactics: Structured data (Schema.org) is more important than ever for "teaching" LLMs about your content.

• AI Workflow: Using AI for "Technical Content Engineering" to ensure data is machine-readable.
