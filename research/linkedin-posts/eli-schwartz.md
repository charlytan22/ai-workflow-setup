Summary: Eli Schwartz, author of "Product-Led SEO," argues that "AI-Proof Content Beats Generic SEO." He emphasizes that the era of producing thin content for single keywords is over, and the focus must shift to comprehensive answers that genuinely solve user problems.

Key Takeaways:

• "Generic" SEO, which relies on keyword stuffing and superficial content, is no longer effective in an AI-dominated search landscape.

• The new paradigm requires focusing on "Product-Led SEO," where content is designed to solve real user problems and align with product strategy.

• Building "SEO Products" (e.g., calculators, interactive tools, data hubs ) that serve as authoritative resources is a more effective strategy, as AI tools are likely to link to and cite such valuable assets.

How This Applies To AI-Powered SEO Content Production:
B2B SaaS companies should re-evaluate their content strategy to prioritize depth, utility, and problem-solving. Instead of merely generating keyword-rich articles, they should invest in creating valuable, interactive resources that naturally attract links and citations from both users and AI. This approach ensures content is not only AI-proof but also drives tangible product engagement and user acquisition.
